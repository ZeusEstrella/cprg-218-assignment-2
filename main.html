World War II Major Events Timeline

Explore key moments that shaped the course of World War II, the deadliest conflict in human history.

Introduction

World War II, which lasted from 1939 to 1945, was a global conflict that involved most of the world's nations. It was marked by significant military campaigns, technological innovations, and profound human suffering. The war altered the course of history, reshaping borders, economies, and societies across the globe.

This timeline highlights some of the major events that occurred during the war, from its beginning with the invasion of Poland to the pivotal battles and strategies that led to its conclusion.

On September 1, 1939, Germany invaded Poland, prompting Britain and France to declare war on Germany.

In 1940, Germany invaded Norway on April 8, and Winston Churchill became British Prime Minister on May 10. The same day, Germany invaded Belgium.

On June 22, 1941, Hitler launched Operation Barbarossa, invading the Soviet Union.

In 1942, the Battle of Midway occurred from June 4 to June 7, marking a turning point in the Pacific Theater.
